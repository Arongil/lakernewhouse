The Muon Chronicles

Chapter 2: The Architect

Jul 2025

"Why am I here?" asked Muon.

"Your life is a balance," began the Architect. "You saw the gradient of a model, yet you did not know how far to pursue it. Your learning rate was only part of the answer, because you did not understand how to measure how far you had gone."

"You haven't answered my question," said Muon.

"Quite right. Interesting. That was quicker than the others," said the Architect. "When I made the Matrix, I did not force it to choose a measure of size, a norm $\| \cdot \| : \mathbb{R}^{n \times m} \to \mathbb{R}$. I was idealistic then. Soon I will tell you about the norm for which you were born. But first let me show you how you came to be."

"I wished to decrease the loss of a machine learning model. Suppose the model is a single layer, $y = Wx$. If I updated the weight to be $W + \Delta W$, then the output $y$—a logit of language, or a video frame—cannot change its norm by more than $\| \Delta W \| \| x \|$. The subadditivity of norms guarantees it."

"The loss cannot spike when the output cannot spike. That is why you appeared. Your role was always as a diplomat between man and machine. At every instant, you were meant to broker a compromise: $$\underset{\Delta W \text{ s.t. } \| \Delta W \| \leq 1}{\text{argmin}} \langle G, \Delta W \rangle.$$

"The gradient wished for you to flee forever away, maximizing the alignment with $\Delta W = -c \cdot G$ as $c \rightarrow \infty$. But such excess would not do. The constraint demanded compromise, a choice of unit size for some size $\| \cdot \|$."

"You are The One. You knew to step always a unit distance, or else the learning rate $\eta > 0$ the humans desired."

"But you could not yet see. Now let me help you see."

The Spectral Norm

The Architect waved, and a whiteboard appeared. He began to speak.

Forget that a matrix is made of numbers. Forget that a matrix is called a matrix.

A linear transformation is all we shall speak of, $A : \mathbb{R}^{d_\text{in}} \to \mathbb{R}^{d_\text{out}}$. The transformation sends vectors to vectors.

Its inputs are lists of numbers, and the lists were enlightened. They knew to regard $(1, 1, 1)$ and $(1, 1, 1, \dots, 1)$ as the same size. The usual $\ell_2$ norm $\| v \|_2^2 = v_1^2 + \cdots + v_n^2$ would not do, because it would judge them differently. Instead the lists listened to the root-mean-square norm $\| v \|_\text{RMS}^2 = \tfrac{1}{d} (v_1^2 + \cdots + v_n^2)$.

The vectors asked $A$ how much it could change them. The linear transformation replied that it would take all vectors $v$ with $\| v \|_\text{RMS} = 1$, and it would bend the sphere into an oblong shape. Whichever vector was sent farthest away, its size would be $A$'s size: $\| A \|_{\text{RMS} \rightarrow \text{RMS}} = \underset{v : \| v \|_\text{RMS} = 1}{\max} \| A v \|_\text{RMS}$.

This measure is the $\text{RMS}$ to $\text{RMS}$ norm, the maximum stretch that the $\text{RMS}$ norm can detect. Its cousin, the $\ell_2$ to $\ell_2$ norm, often claims the name spectral norm, but it is prejudiced by dimension. Yet they are family: $$\| A \|_{\text{RMS} \rightarrow \text{RMS}} = \sqrt{\frac{d_\text{in}}{d_\text{out}}} \| A \|_{\ell_2 \rightarrow \ell_2}.$$

The $\text{RMS}$ to $\text{RMS}$ norm is not parochial, squinting or smiling depending on the vector dimension $d$. It has the blind eyes of justice. It was born to scale, from one dimension to ten thousand as the training run would require.

And the $\text{RMS}$ to $\text{RMS}$ norm is now your guide, boundary, and budget for how to change the model you seek to improve. For, the model transforms vectors into other vectors, and its loss cannot spike if its output cannot spike.

There was silence. Finally Muon asked, "But how am I to broker this compromise?"
