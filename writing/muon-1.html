The Muon Chronicles

Chapter 1: Into the Matrix

Jul 2025

This is the first chapter of the Muon Chronicles, a practitioner's guide about the Muon optimizer. The Muon optimizer is a new optimizer for training deep learning models that people are very excited about right now because it's shown ~2x speedups from 100M up to 1T parameter scale (see 1, 2, 3). Want to see it for yourself?

To understand Muon, the first step is to enter the Matrix.

Normally we think of neural network gradients as vectors of unrelated numbers. Not anymore.

A neural network does matrix multiplications (lots of them). Therefore Muon views the gradients as matrices, too, unlike Adam which treats every parameter independently. And in the Matrix, there is a lot more to do.

Neo, The One

Optimizers are constantly struggling against the fundamental tension: they want the loss to go down as fast as possible, but the model's gradient is only accurate exactly where it is measured. To step a finite distance is always a leap of faith.

And it's not clear how to use information from how big the gradient is.

If the gradient is 10x as large, should we step 10x as far?

Imagine a loss landscape, like a parabola $y=(x/100)^2$. The gradients will be small, but the model should take big steps. If the loss were $y=(100x)^2$, the gradients would be big, but the model should take small steps.

Many optimizers resolve this tension with some form of the following medicine: no matter how giant or tiny the gradient is, move a fixed distance of one. Move in the direction of the gradient, but do not listen to its size.

The optimizer wishes it could step forever toward the gradient. Self-denial strengthens the optimizer.

The elementwise optimizers have a limited menu for how to take this medicine. The simplest way is SignSGD:

  • Instead of SGD: $W_{n+1} = W_n - \eta \cdot G_{n+1}$
  • Do SignSGD: $W_{n+1} = W_n - \eta \cdot \text{sign}(G_{n+1})$

where $\eta > 0$ is the leap-of-faith parameter (commonly known as the learning rate).

The Lion optimizer is very nearly SignSGD with momentum, to smooth out the gradient estimate.

The Adam optimizer—king of the realm—reduces to SignSGD when its momentum is turned off. In its general form, Adam tracks two momentum buffers with different time scales and makes updates akin to $\frac{G}{\sqrt{G^2}} \approx \text{sign}(G)$ so that its update's entries hover near the golden compromise of One.

But Muon... he has entered the Matrix. He does not see entries of a matrix. He sees the matrix itself.
