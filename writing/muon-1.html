Chapter 1: Into the Matrix

Jul 2025

This is the first chapter of the Muon Chronicles, a practitioner's guide to the Muon optimizer—told as a story to make it stickier to remember. People are very excited about Muon right now because it's shown ~2x speedups training models from 100M to 1T parameter scale (see 1, 2, 3). We'll walk through everything from why it works to a line-by-line annotation of its PyTorch implementation. Want to see it for yourself?

"To understand Muon, the first step is to enter the Matrix."
—Not quite Morpheus

The blue pill says that a gradient's entries are unrelated numbers. Not anymore.

A neural network does matrix multiplications (lots of them). Therefore Muon sees the gradients as matrices, unlike Adam which treats every parameter independently. And in the Matrix, there is a lot more you can do.

Neo, The One

Optimizers are constantly struggling against the fundamental tension: they want the loss to go down far, but the model's gradient is only accurate exactly where it is measured. To step a finite distance is always a leap of faith.

And when making that leap, it's not clear how to use how big the gradient is.

If the gradient is 10x as large, should we step 10x as far?

Quite the opposite. If the loss were $y=(x/100)^2$, the gradients would be small, but the model should take big steps. If the loss were $y=(100x)^2$, the gradients would be big, but the model should take small steps.

Many optimizers resolve this tension with some form of the following medicine: no matter how giant or tiny the gradient is, move a fixed distance of one. Move in the direction of the gradient, but do not listen to its size.

The elementwise optimizers have a limited menu for how to take this medicine. The simplest way is SignSGD:

  • Instead of SGD: $W_{n+1} = W_n - \eta \cdot G_{n+1}$
  • Do SignSGD: $W_{n+1} = W_n - \eta \cdot \text{sign}(G_{n+1})$

where $\eta > 0$ is the leap-of-faith parameter (commonly known as the learning rate).

The Adam optimizer—king of the realm—reduces to SignSGD when its momentum is turned off. In its general form, Adam tracks two momentum buffers with different time scales. Its updates are akin to $\frac{G}{\sqrt{G^2}} \approx \text{sign}(G)$, and its update entries hover near the golden compromise of One.

But Muon... he has entered the Matrix. He does not see entries of a matrix. He sees the matrix itself.
