Tips, Tricks, and Guide to Laker

Hello and welcome to this facetious (but possibly helpful) introduction to me.

1. Outlook on Life

There's a lot to do to secure a prosperous, scientific, and democratic future. Viktor Frankl often said that to complement the Statue of Liberty on the east coast, we should erect a Statue of Responsibility on the west.

What I want to do with my life is to make a big, positive impact on society. And it is more fun to do it together. Like Shakespeare plays or the U.S. Constitution, this goal lacks stage directions. Lucky for us, many very smart people have written down the great ideas that our society is founded on. That is why I love reading and learning. As MIT's legendary professor Bruno Verdini once said, we're capable of so much loftier dreams than we imagine.

All the while, friends, family, and laughter are what make life worth living. Let's not forget that among big ideas.

2. How to Talk to Me

Nice to meet you! I'm sure we'll enjoy speaking. One characteristic of favorite conversations is that we get past small talk. What do you love? How do you spend your time? Have you learned anything recently? I tend to bond first on an intellectual level, co-sculpting ideas for how to approach life. I'm usually searching for ways to fulfill my outlook on life, which might mean empowering you with an excitement and sense of responsibility, if I can. I'm interested in almost everything, including math, physics, philosophy, history, law, language, education, and AI.

3. Living Together

3.1 Dietary Habits

I prefer to eat Kosher. That means two rules: no pork or shellfish; no milk+meat at the same time.

Example: Noncommutativity of Dairy and Meat

Let's say we eat chicken burritos for lunch. After happily enjoying, I will not be able to break into ice cream until a few hours later. However, if we eat the ice cream first, I could eat a chicken burrito after 20 minutes, or after drinking a glass of water. Why? Meat gets stuck in teeth, while milk doesn't. Or so rabbis debate.

In general, I like all foods. How easy! One exception is that I prefer food with no added sugar. Whether it is yogurt, cereal, or salad dressing, an unsweetened variety is my preference. American grocery stores can make this hard.

Some favorite foods include oatmeal, peanut butter, Gruyere cheese, strawberries, and frozen mangos. For drinks, I'm simple: water is my favorite. I like milk and tea, too. I don't drink coffee or alcohol, so far at least!

3.2 Bedtime Habits

I prefer to sleep around 10pm. Before bed each night, I write an entry in a journal I've kept since February, 2022. I often use a fan or other white noise device and an eye mask to help fall asleep. If we're going to bed at different times, I will walk softly and close doors quietly to not wake you up; I would appreciate if you did the same.
